MobiArch 2025

Workshop on Mobility in the Evolving Internet Architecture to be held in conjunction with MobiCom 2025

Hongkong, China, 8th Nov., 2025

Call for Papers

The last two decades have witnessed an extremely fast evolution of network technologies and a steep growth in mobile data traffic. According to the latest edition of the Cisco Visual Networking Index (VNI), global mobile data traffic is projected to increase nearly threefold from 2020 to 2025, reaching 184 exabytes per month by 2025. This growth is being fueled by factors such as the proliferation of mobile devices, the increasing availability of high-speed mobile networks, and the growing adoption of data-intensive mobile applications. Meanwhile, the integration of AI-driven technologies, including large models and foundation models, is significantly enhancing the intelligence and adaptability of networked systems, offering new opportunities for research innovation and development.

Globally, 5G network deployment is rapidly moving from trials to early commercialization, promising higher data speeds, lower latency, and improved throughput to support more simultaneous connections without disruptions. However, several challenges remain unresolved, driving further research efforts. Emerging applications are pushing quality-of-service requirements to extreme levels, while mobile edge computing faces challenges in handling user mobility in highly dynamic environments. Additionally, the adoption of open and intelligent network architectures is enabling greater flexibility and interoperability. Large-scale AI models are increasingly being integrated into mobile networks, enhancing decision-making, optimizing resource allocation, and enabling predictive analytics. These developments highlight the need for in-depth research on next-generation mobile Internet architectures, leveraging AI-native solutions to address scalability, efficiency, and intelligent service provisioning.

The MobiArch 2025 workshop will focus on the technical architecture issues for next-generation mobile Internet, to tackle the aforementioned challenges. MobiArch 2025 welcomes submissions from both researchers and practitioners from academia and industry that explore the latest developments in mobile communication technologies and architectures. In addition, we encourage work-in-progress and position papers that describe highly original ideas, present new directions, or have the potential to generate insightful, provocative discussion at the workshop.

Topics of Interest

Key research topics include but are not limited to:

  • 6G and AI-Enhanced Wireless Networks
  • Cloud-Edge-Device Collaboration for Future Mobile Systems
  • Digital Twins and Virtualized Architectures in Mobile Networks
  • Collaborative Intelligence and Federated Learning for Mobile Systems
  • Cyber-Physical Integration in Next-Generation Wireless Networks
  • Privacy-Preserving Technologies, Blockchain, and Decentralized Security
  • Immersive AR/VR and Extended Reality (XR) over Mobile Networks
  • Sustainable, Green, and Energy-Efficient Mobile Networking
  • Quantum-Secured and Ultra-Reliable Communication for Mobile Systems
  • Trustworthy, Robust, and Explainable AI in Wireless Communications
  • Edge AI, Real-Time Decision Making, and Autonomous Network Optimization
  • QoS/QoE Optimization for AI-Driven and Next-Gen Mobile Services
  • Scalable Large Model Deployment and Optimization in Mobile Networks
  • Emerging Applications and Services in Intelligent Mobile Networks
